Week 1 Research

Week 1 Discussion / Research Questions

Question 1

1. Less obvious perks of using 'live' data resources include logins and payments. How could Instagram be used in these two scenarios?

So long as I understood the GoTo Training correctly we were supposed to put Instagram in place of Twitter. Instagram could use these two scenarios by say using Facebook for logins and PayPal for payments. This one prevents the user from having to have multiple different logins if they are using their facebook account to login with. And PayPal is a trusted source that the user can use to make purchases.

Question 2

2. Visit Programmableweb.com and search for JSON-P data sources. Why would we need JSON-P, specifically?

Because it allows us to request data from a server in a different domain, instead of the information having to be on the same domain.

Question 3

3. Based on JSON-P search results: what data resource(s) do you think might pair well with which types of Native device features?

MapQuest Geocoding may be one data resource that may pair well with the gps native device feature for my app. Say my user wants to mark a location of a store they were at to the list item they added, this may be a great feature to work for it.

Question 4

4. Please describe any design and functionality intentions you may have for your Demo app components this month.

So I decided to incorporate my app from our previous class for this month. It's a list app, not necessarily a "TO Do" list app but just a list, either groceries, supplies, anything really you may need to make a list of and where to go for that list. I also feel that using the barcode camera feature would be good for this app, If I can make ti work the where the user if out at the store sees something they want to add to their list, they can use the barcode scanner to add that item to the list and hopefully will add of the information, price, etc to it also.

Question 5

5. Briefly describe some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc.

Some past lessons learned that I use now, would definitely be planning out my workflow, and time management for that. Coming up with the idea for the app, then setting up a design workflow of how I want that app to look. Then going to do some research on similar apps, and then coding.

Week 2 Research

Week 2 Discussion / Research Questions

Question 1

1. What are some of the ways that you could change your HTML on your Demo app to be more accessible?(Remember, you can use the Section tag with jQuery, for instance).

Some ways that I could change my HTML on my demo app to be more accessible would be to change all my div tags with data-role attribute of page to a section tag, this will make my code easier to read for other developers as well as myself since I use the single HTML file with multiple pages.

Question 2

2. Visit a video service on the web (like YouTube) and search for "Screen Reader Demo" and "Mobile Screen Reader" - what are some of the common frustrations (or common support mechanisms) that you see among the examples that are given - how could you fix them (or use them)?

I watched several videos on both the Screen Reader and Mobile Screen Reader, and both seemed to be very well geared towards people with visual impairments. One thing that did bother me a bit was how it was read, but with the upgrading of the read back on phones, making it sound less robotic would be a plus. Another thing that could be a frustration is not using semantic coding ie: alt tags, One way we as developers when coding especially for people with impairments is making sure we are coding semantically.

Question 3

3. Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

1st device is the iPhone accessibility features: You have the zoom and large text feature for people with poor vision, for people with hearing problems, there is the LED flash for alerts, and the Hearing Aids feature. The iPhone offers other features for impairments as well.

2nd device is the Android accessibility features: for visual, physical or age related limitations Android offers features such as text-to-speech, haptic feedback, trackball and directional pad navigation as well as numerous other features

Question 4

4. Mobile Apps: Please list two apps (either Android or iOS, or both) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

2 apps made specifically to support Accessibility are the iOS apps called iBlink Radio for the visually impaired and Big Clock aslo for the visually impaired.

Week 3 Research

Week 3 Discussion / Research Questions

Question 1

1. What are the ‘top selling’ app categories in the US? Japan? And Great Britain?

So to start off, I’m going to give the most popular Apple App store categories as of July 2013 worldwide – they are Games 16.9%, Education 10.83% and Entertainment 8.70%. (Statisca, 2013). Now looking at app categories by country I found that the top selling category in the U.S. is games, in Japan the top selling app category is games and in Great Britain the top selling app category is games as well, and the #1 top selling app is Asphalt 8. (AppAnnie, 2013)

Question 2

2. Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

What I gathered from my research based on the 3 countries listed above, is that we share a universal love for gaming, especially mobile gaming. I know I find myself, while on break at work, taking out my phone and playing a few rounds of Words with Friends, or Star Trek Rivals. It’s a great way to pass time, and have some fun with friends during a busy work week.

Question 3

3. What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

Looking through various cell phone services data plans in the US average around $15-$20 a month for 200MBs, In Japan the average is about $5 a month for 0.5MB and in the UK the average is about $8 for 5120MBs.

Question 4

4. And, finally, what would be your suggested strategy in project type and global market choice? Feel free to have a first and second choice.

My strategy after doing some research definitely leads me to believe that mobile gaming and mobile business apps are the way to go. But in doing so as developers we should lead towards developing apps that have low data usage or that focus on the users local storage. Possibly when having updates to the app, instead of automatic updates, have the user choose when to update, so they can choose to be over a wireless connection instead of using their data plan for the updates.
